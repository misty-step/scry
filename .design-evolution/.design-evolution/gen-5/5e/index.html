Question 2
\\n

\\n Which class of antibiotics is most commonly associated with QT interval prolongation and risk of torsades de pointes?\\n

\\n
\\n
\\n \\n \\n \\n \\n
\\n
\\n
\\n \\n \\n
\\n
\\n
\\n \\n Incorrect\\n
\\n
\\n \\n
\\n
\\n
Your Answer
\\n
Beta-lactams (penicillins, cephalosporins)
\\n
\\n
\\n
Correct Answer
\\n
Macrolides (erythromycin, azithromycin)
\\n
\\n
\\n \\n
\\n
Clinical Significance
\\n

\\n Macrolides block the hERG potassium channel, delaying repolarization and prolonging the QT interval. \\n This effect is most pronounced with erythromycin and clarithromycin; azithromycin carries lower but still significant risk. \\n Always review concurrent medications that may also prolong QT (antipsychotics, antiarrhythmics) before prescribing. \\n This knowledge is critical for patient safety in outpatient and ICU settings.\\n
