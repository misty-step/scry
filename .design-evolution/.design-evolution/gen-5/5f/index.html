What was the primary cause of the Panic of 1837 in the United States?

A — The Specie Circular and the dismantling of the Second Bank of the United States
\\n
\\n
\\n \\n
\\n
Explanation
\\n
\\n

The Panic of 1837 was primarily caused by Andrew Jackson\'s economic policies, particularly the dismantling of the Second Bank of the United States and the Specie Circular (1836), which required payment for government land in gold or silver rather than paper money.

\\n

Without the Second Bank to regulate state banks, credit expansion spiraled out of control. When the Specie Circular was implemented, it drained specie from eastern banks to western land offices, causing banks to collapse and credit to contract. Martin Van Buren inherited this economic disaster when he took office in March 1837.
